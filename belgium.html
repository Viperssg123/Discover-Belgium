About Belgium

A Kingdom of Contrasts

Belgium is a small but remarkably diverse federal state in Western Europe. Bordered by France, Germany, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, and the North Sea, it punches far above its weight in culture, cuisine, and diplomacy — hosting both NATO and the European Union headquarters.

The country is divided into three regions: Flanders (Dutch-speaking north), Wallonia (French-speaking south), and Brussels (officially bilingual capital).

Cities Worth Every Step

Capital

Brussels

The cosmopolitan capital blends Art Nouveau architecture with EU politics, world-class museums, and legendary street food.

Medieval Gem

Culture & Traditions

Life, Savoured
Slowly

Belgium's culture is a rich tapestry woven from Flemish, Walloon, and Germanic threads. It's a nation that perfected the art of the good life: world-class beer, sublime chocolate, crispy frites, and festivals that fill entire towns with joy.

🧇

Belgian Waffles

Light, crispy, and dusted with powdered sugar — the liège and Brussels varieties are beloved worldwide.

🍫

Fine Chocolate

Belgium houses over 2,000 chocolate shops. Pralines were invented here in 1912 by Jean Neuhaus.

🍺

Beer Culture

Over 400 Belgian beer varieties are recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage.

🎭

Festivals

From the Ghent Festival to Tomorrowland, Belgium celebrates with unmatched energy and creativity.

A Rich Past

Centuries of History

57 BC
Roman Conquest

Julius Caesar conquers the "Belgae" tribes, as described in his Gallic Wars, making the region a prosperous Roman province.

1302
Battle of the Golden Spurs

Flemish militia defeat the French cavalry, becoming a defining moment of Flemish identity and national pride.

1568–1648
Eighty Years' War

The Spanish Netherlands era reshapes the region's borders and identity through decades of religious and political conflict.

1830
Independence

The Belgian Revolution results in independence from the Netherlands. Leopold I becomes the first King of the Belgians.

1914–1918
World War I

Belgium heroically resists German invasion. The "brave little Belgium" narrative gains international sympathy and admiration.

1958
Expo 58 & European Community

Brussels hosts the World Expo and becomes the de facto capital of the emerging European project — a role it holds to this day.

1993
Federal State

Belgium officially becomes a federal state with three regions, three communities, and three official languages.

Practical Guide

Travel Smart

01

Best Time to Visit

April–June and September–October offer mild weather and fewer crowds. July brings festivals like Tomorrowland. Avoid rainy November.

02

Getting Around

Belgium has an excellent rail network. The train between Brussels, Bruges, Ghent, and Antwerp takes under an hour. Cycling is king in Flanders.

03

Language Tips

Dutch in Flanders, French in Wallonia, German in the east. English is widely spoken in cities. Learn "merci" and "dankjewel" — locals appreciate it.

04

Currency & Budget

Belgium uses the Euro. It's moderately priced. Budget €60–100/day for a comfortable trip including accommodation, food, and transport.

05

Local Customs

Belgians value punctuality, privacy, and good food. Greet with a single cheek kiss. Don't rush meals — dining is a social ritual here.

06

Must-Try Foods

Moules-frites, speculoos biscuits, jenever gin, Liège waffles, and Trappist ales. Skip the tourist traps — find a local brasserie.
